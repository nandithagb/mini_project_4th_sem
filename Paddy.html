Paddy growing situations
  • Nala irrigated areas (zone - 4, 5 and parts of 6) zone - 6, Krishnarajanagar achukattu areas (Mandya, Mysore districts and some parts) and zone - 4 Vani villas and Badhra achukattu (Hiriyur and parts of Davangere areas).
  • Tank irrigated areas (zone - 5 and parts of 6) zone - 5 Kolar and Chikkaballapur, Ramnagar, Tumkur and Bangalore districts and in zone- 6 some parts paddy is grown under tank bed areas.
  • Saline soil areas (zone - 4 and parts of 6) rice growing areas occur in low laying patches largely in zone- 4 and 6 .In these soil, paddy is also growing.
  • Punaji paddy (zone - 5 and parts of 6)  is grown in zone - 5 (Kolar, Chikkaballapur, Ramanagar, Tumkur and Bangalore districts) and in a very limited area in zone- 6 (Kollegala and Chamarajanagar taluks). This crop is grown directly with the onset of rains in May and June, and maintained as rainfed paddy until August later on water is let-in and subsequently continued as wet rice.
  • Semi malnad areas (zone - 7) 40% of paddy is growing under irrigation facilities available through canal, tank and bore wells and remainin.g 60% of paddy is growing under rainfed condition.
  • Malnad areas (zone - 9) Chikkamagaluru, Shivamoga, Kodagu and in Hassan districts paddy growing under rainfed condition.
  • Coastal (zone - 10) Dakshina Kannada, Udupi districts, paddy is the main crop. High rainfall received during monsoon season, paddy is growing in medium and low laying fields.
