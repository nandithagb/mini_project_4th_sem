Sustainable paddy production through integrated nutrient management (INM)

In Karnataka Cauvery water shed areas, the soils are sand mixed with clay particles hence the available nutrients to the crops are low.
Also these areas only single cropping is practicing. FYM/ compost and green manure application is very limited hence the soil health is degrading day by day.
Under these circumstances in order to obtain sustainable paddy yield soil health management is very important.
Based on soil fertility and production, INM study was taken at Zonal Agricultural Research Station V.C. farm Mandya for longer period of 15 yrs and drawn conclusions.

Use of different organic manures based on its nutrients available for paddy cultivation

Based on above table data, 10 ton FYM or 5 t. green leaf manure fertilizers application can save 50% of nutrients through organic manure.
Out of total nutrients, application of 50% of in-organic fertilizers, nitrogen, super phosphate and potassium and remaining 50% nutrients through organic manures application can sustain the paddy yield.
Application of I.N.M during first 4 - 5 yrs period as per recommendation though average yield obtained, after 5 yrs the yield was increased significantly as per the research findings.
Through I.N.M management, the yield and soil health improvement is observed.
About 2 times higher net profit is obtained, hence the famers can use the available organic manures based on following nutrients application to the paddy cultivation
1. Apply 4 t. of FYM to the soil before transplanting and followed by ploughing and application of 20 kg nitrogen, 10 kg phosphorus and 10 kg potash, in-organic fertilizers per acre.
2.Application of green manure crops like chambe, sesbania, pongemia leaves etc. 2 t. to the soil, allow 2 - 3 weeks for decomposition and then apply 20 kg nitrogen, 10 kg phosphorous and 10 kg potash, in-organic fertilizers per acre.
3.If FYM/compost and green manure fertilizers availability is not adequately available, apply 2 t. FYM/compost to the green manure crops growing plots. After 45 - 50 days of green manure crops are to be incorporated into the soil by ploughing. 2 -3 weeks after its decomposition in the soil apply 20 kg nitrogen, 10 kg phosphorous and 10 kg potash in-organic fertilizers per acre.
